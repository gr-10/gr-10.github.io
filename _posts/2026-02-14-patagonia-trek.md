I've done a lot of hikes. Snowdon in Wales, the Cinque Terre coastal trail, the Annapurna circuit — but nothing prepared me for Patagonia. Torres del Paine sits at the end of the world, and it feels like it.

<div class="post-img-block">🌄</div>

## Getting There

From Puerto Natales, the gateway town, a 2-hour bus deposits you at the park entrance. I stayed three nights beforehand to acclimatise and nail down my refugio reservations — book these 6 months ahead, no kidding.

My pack weighed 11 kg with all camping gear. I'd trained weighted-vest carries for eight weeks prior. Still wasn't fully ready.

## Day 1 — Mirador Las Torres

The classic start: a brutal 4-hour ascent to the iconic three granite spires. The final push — a loose boulder field at altitude — tests your legs and your patience. But the moment you crest that last boulder field and see the towers reflected in the milky-green tarn below… all suffering dissolves instantly.

> "You don't hike Patagonia. Patagonia hikes you."

<div class="post-img-block">🗻</div>

## Days 2–3 — The French Valley

If Las Torres is the postcard, Valle del Francés is the soul. You walk through ancient lenga beech forest as glaciers calve overhead with cannon-crack booms. I stood frozen for 20 minutes just watching ice fall. Time moves differently out there.

### Physical Demands

Average daily distance: 18–22 km with 1,200 m of elevation gain. My legs were smashed by Day 3. Lessons learned:

- Trekking poles are not optional — they save your knees on descents.
- Carry electrolytes. The physical output is enormous.
- Altitude hits differently when you're carrying 11 kg. Build that base at home.

## Day 4 — Grey Glacier

The third branch of the W leads to Glaciar Grey, a 270 km² slab of blue ice calving into a turquoise lake. I kayaked among icebergs the size of buses. No filter. No hyperbole. Just ice and silence and wonder.

## What Patagonia Taught Me

Every hard thing in the gym — every set you want to quit, every early morning alarm — is training for moments like this. Physical preparation isn't vanity; it's the price of admission to the world's most extraordinary places.

> "The mountains don't care about your excuses. They only reward your preparation."

## Practical Notes

- **Best time:** November–March (Southern Hemisphere summer)
- **Bookings:** CONAF permits + refugios sell out fast — book 6 months early
- **Gear essentials:** Waterproof everything, gaiters, quality merino base layers
- **Training:** 12+ weeks of hiking with elevation, weighted carries, and high daily step counts
