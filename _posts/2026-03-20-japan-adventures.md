Japan had been on my list for years. I finally landed at Narita on a crisp February morning, jet-lagged but buzzing with anticipation. Ten days felt generous until I opened my itinerary and realised I'd crammed in Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka, and Hakone — and somehow planned a trail run around Mount Kurama.

<div class="post-img-block">🗾</div>

## Tokyo: Running the Megalopolis

My favourite way to understand a city is to run it. Tokyo rewarded this spectacularly. I laced up at 6 am every morning and hit the Imperial Palace running course — a flat 5 km loop packed with locals of all ages.

After runs I'd collapse into a steaming bowl of ramen. My go-to spots were the underground ramen alley in Shinjuku and a tiny 8-seater tonkotsu joint near Shimokitazawa. Japan's food is genuinely gym-friendly: high-protein, clean, and deeply satisfying.

> "Train hard, eat well, explore everything — Japan makes all three embarrassingly easy."

## Kyoto: Temple Treks & Mountain Trails

Kyoto is where the soul of Japan lives. I could have spent the whole trip wandering Fushimi Inari's thousand torii gates at dawn — but I had a trail run to do.

### The Kurama Trail

A 10 km loop from Kibune to Kurama through dense cedar forest. The trail climbs steeply right from the start — quads burning, lungs working, but the silence and dappled light made every step meditative. At the top, a small shrine. At the bottom, a bowl of soba noodles by a mountain stream. Perfect.

## Osaka: Eat, Lift, Repeat

Osaka's mantra is *kuidaore* — eat until you drop. I found a local gym near Namba, grabbed a day pass, and kept my strength work going. Japanese gyms are clean, quiet, and serious. No one talks to you; everyone just trains.

Evenings were for Dotonbori's street food chaos: takoyaki, yakitori, matcha everything. I stopped counting macros and just enjoyed every bite.

## Fitness Tips for Travelling Japan

- **Walking counts:** I averaged 20,000 steps daily just from sightseeing. Active recovery is real.
- **Konbini for fuel:** 7-Eleven and Lawson carry boiled eggs, edamame, and onigiri — perfect high-protein snacks.
- **Day-pass gyms:** Most cities have chain gyms (Anytime Fitness, JOYFIT) that accept walk-ins for ¥1,000–1,500.
- **Trail apps:** Download Yamap before you go — it's Japan's best offline trail GPS.

## Final Thoughts

Japan is the rare destination that feeds both the wanderer and the athlete in you. The culture demands respect, the food is phenomenal, and the trails are world-class. I left lighter in spirit, heavier in luggage (too many books and matcha kits), and already planning my return.

> "Japan doesn't just visit you. It stays with you — in the smell of cedar, the taste of ramen, the echo of a lonely shrine bell."
