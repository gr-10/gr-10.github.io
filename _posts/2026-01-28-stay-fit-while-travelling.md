The number one excuse I hear from fellow travellers is: "I can't train — I'm travelling." I used to say it too. Then I realised the excuse was a lie I was telling myself, and I stopped.

Here's everything I've figured out over years of training across four continents, dozens of hotels, hostels, and Airbnbs.

## The Mindset Shift

Consistency beats perfection. A 20-minute bodyweight circuit in your hotel room is infinitely better than skipping because you couldn't find the perfect gym. Release the idea that every session needs to be a gym session.

> "The best workout is the one you actually do."

## My Go-To Bodyweight Routine (40 min)

This works anywhere — hotel room, park, hostel rooftop. No equipment needed.

### Warm-Up (5 min)

- World's greatest stretch — 5 reps each side
- Hip circles — 10 each direction
- Arm crossovers and thoracic rotations

### Main Circuit — 4 Rounds

- Push-up variations × 15 (standard, wide, diamond)
- Bulgarian split squat × 12 each leg (use a chair or bed)
- Pike push-ups × 10
- Single-leg Romanian deadlift × 10 each side
- Plank to down-dog × 12
- Jump squats × 15 (sub regular squats if noise is a concern)

### Core Finisher (5 min)

- Dead bug — 3 × 10 reps
- Side plank — 30 sec each side
- Hollow body hold — 3 × 20 sec

## Finding Gyms on the Road

When I do want iron in my hands, here's how I find gyms fast:

- **Anytime Fitness:** Global network, reciprocal access with home membership.
- **Google Maps:** Search "gym near me", sort by rating. Walk in and ask for a day pass — most will say yes.
- **University gyms:** Often open to the public for a small fee and have excellent facilities.
- **Hostel facilities:** Mid-range hostels increasingly have basic gym equipment. Ask at check-in.

## Nutrition on the Road

- **Protein first:** At every meal, I identify the protein source first and build around it.
- **Local markets over restaurants:** Fresh produce, eggs, nuts — cheaper, healthier, and more interesting.
- **Carry protein powder:** A small ziploc of whey travels easily and saves you on busy travel days.
- **Don't stress:** One bad nutrition day doesn't undo months of work. Enjoy the local food.

## The One Non-Negotiable

Sleep. Every time I sacrifice sleep for an extra nightcap or late-night city wander, my training suffers for two days. Jet-lag is real. Prioritise sleep as aggressively as you prioritise sightseeing.

> "You don't get fit in the gym. You get fit when you recover. Protect your sleep like a training asset."

## My Travel Fitness Kit

- Resistance bands (flat and loop) — fit in any bag
- Jump rope — 200 g, packs flat
- Small foam roller (or a lacrosse ball)
- Running shoes that double as gym shoes
- 500 g protein powder in a sealed container

That's it. Everything else is noise. Stay consistent, be creative, and remember — the world is your gym.
