When I walked into a gym for the first time, I had no idea what I was doing. I copied the guy next to me, got the form completely wrong, and barely progressed for 6 months. This guide is everything I wish someone had handed me on day one.

## Why Strength Training?

Strength training isn't just about aesthetics. It builds bone density, improves posture, boosts metabolism, enhances athletic performance, and — crucially for a traveller — prepares your body for physical adventures. Trekking Patagonia or climbing Toubkal is significantly easier when you have a strong posterior chain.

## The Fundamentals: 5 Movement Patterns

Every effective program revolves around these patterns. Master them and you master the gym.

### 1. Push (Horizontal & Vertical)
Bench press, overhead press, push-ups. Develops chest, shoulders, and triceps.

### 2. Pull (Horizontal & Vertical)
Rows, pull-ups, lat pulldown. Develops back and biceps. This is the most underdeveloped pattern for beginners.

### 3. Squat
Back squat, goblet squat, front squat. Quad-dominant lower body strength.

### 4. Hinge
Deadlift, Romanian deadlift, hip thrust. Posterior chain — the most important pattern for injury prevention and athletic performance.

### 5. Carry
Farmer's carry, suitcase carry. Often neglected. Builds total-body stability and grip strength.

> "Get the patterns right first. The weight is secondary. Always."

## A Simple 12-Week Program

3 days per week. Full-body each session. Progressive overload each week.

### Day A

- Barbell back squat — 3 × 5
- Bench press — 3 × 5
- Barbell row — 3 × 5
- Plank — 3 × 30 sec

### Day B

- Deadlift — 1 × 5 (add weight each session)
- Overhead press — 3 × 5
- Chin-ups / Lat pulldown — 3 × 8
- Romanian deadlift — 3 × 8

Alternate A–B–A one week, B–A–B the next. Add 2.5 kg to upper body lifts and 5 kg to lower body lifts each session when you hit all reps cleanly.

## Form Over Everything

Film yourself. You will be horrified. That's good. Watch Jeff Nippard or Alan Thrall on YouTube for technique cues — both are excellent, science-based, and beginner-friendly. Hiring a coach for even 3–4 sessions to learn the main lifts is the best investment you can make.

## Nutrition Basics for Beginners

- **Protein:** Aim for ~1.6–2.2 g per kg of bodyweight. This is the most important variable.
- **Calories:** In a slight surplus (200–300 kcal) to build muscle efficiently as a beginner.
- **Sleep:** 7–9 hours. Muscle is built during recovery, not during the workout.
- **Don't overcomplicate it:** Hit your protein, sleep well, train consistently. The rest is details.

## The Traveller's Caveat

When you're on the road and miss sessions, don't panic. A week off doesn't undo your progress. When you return, drop the weight by 10% and work back up. The body remembers faster than you think.

> "Strength training is a lifelong practice, not a 12-week challenge. The goal is never to be done."

## Resources I Actually Use

- **Books:** *Strength Training Anatomy* by Frédéric Delavier
- **App:** Strong (for logging workouts) — free, simple, excellent
- **YouTube:** Jeff Nippard, Alan Thrall, Squat University

Now go lift something heavy. You'll thank yourself on every future mountain.
