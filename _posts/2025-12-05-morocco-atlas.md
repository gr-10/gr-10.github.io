Morocco assaults your senses the moment you step off the plane. Spice, colour, sound, heat. I'd been warned it was overwhelming. The warning was accurate. It's also utterly intoxicating.

I had 12 days. Three very different Moroccos: the urban labyrinth of Marrakech, the infinite silence of the Sahara, and the cold stone heights of Jebel Toubkal — the highest peak in North Africa at 4,167 m.

## Marrakech: Beautiful Chaos

The medina is an organism. Narrow alleys that double back on themselves, donkeys carrying cement blocks, argan oil vendors, leather tanneries stinking beautifully, rooftop cafés looking out over terracotta domes. I got lost every single day and found something remarkable each time.

Fitness-wise, Marrakech is a walking city. I averaged 18,000 steps daily. I found a hammam near Bab Doukkala — steam room, body scrub, total recovery. I went three times.

> "The medina doesn't have a map. Neither do the best adventures."

## The Sahara: Merzouga

A 9-hour drive through the Atlas Mountains deposits you in Merzouga, at the edge of Erg Chebbi — enormous sand dunes rising from flat desert like orange waves.

### Camel Trek + Desert Camp

I rode a camel for two hours into the dunes as the sun set. The silence at night in the Sahara is absolute. No light pollution. The Milky Way overhead is not a photograph — it's a ceiling of light. I lay awake until 2 am refusing to sleep through it.

Pre-sunrise the next morning, I climbed the tallest dune barefoot. 200 m of soft sand. Legs burning, lungs full of cool desert air, watching the sky turn from black to violet to burning orange. Worth every step.

## Jebel Toubkal: The Summit

Toubkal is technically a non-technical ascent — no ropes required — but at 4,167 m, altitude is the real challenge. I hired a local guide, Hassan, whose family has guided the mountain for three generations.

### Day 1 — Imlil to Refuge du Toubkal (3,207 m)

Four hours of steady climbing through walnut orchards and rocky switchbacks. The refuge is basic but warm. I drank three litres of water, ate a mountain of couscous, and slept by 9 pm.

### Day 2 — Summit Day

3:30 am start. Headtorch, layers, trekking poles. The ascent to the summit is 960 m of relentless scree and boulder. Two hours of grinding. At 7:15 am, I stood on the roof of North Africa.

Hassan shook my hand and handed me a thermos of mint tea. It was the best cup of tea I've ever had.

## Fitness Notes for Morocco

- **Toubkal prep:** At minimum, 8 weeks of progressive hill walking with a loaded pack. Acclimatisation day in Imlil is essential.
- **Hydration in the desert:** 4+ litres per day. Rehydration salts are your best friend.
- **Moroccan food:** Tagine and couscous are great fuel — complex carbs, protein from legumes, healthy fats from olive oil.
- **Hammam:** The traditional steam bath is legitimately excellent recovery. Do it.

> "Every summit is just the beginning of the next descent. And every descent leads to the next mountain."
